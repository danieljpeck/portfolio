Problems with connecting

What is it to stay relevant?

    tl;dr

  • There is little to know recognition and presence of Spring Open Studio

  • There is no centralized strategy for promotion and operations

Currently the artists are facing the problem of stagnation. There aren’t new people going to the Hunter’s Point Shipyard Artists events. The artists are not doing any self promotion, and the current promotion of their Open Studio event is in the hands of their non-profit sponsor. As the artists are not very connected to the outside world they need a new process of promotion, connection, and interaction to a wider audience.

What the artists know

We have a website?

    tl;dr

  • The website doesn't direct users to events or anything really

  • Spring Open Studio model is outdated and unattractive to new audiences

To discover both the latent problems and current climate around the Hunter’s Point Shipyard, my partner and I conducted surveys, interviews, and secondary research to discover the actual needs and behaviors of possible HPSA event goers, as well as the artists themselves. I found interesting patterns in how people attend events, and the importance of familiar event-going models. The potential users also wanted to have more engaging and informative atmosphere and features at the event. The artists, however, were confused or did not know the HPSA’s online presence, including the website and all promotion. They conducted all promotion by cold emails.



Synthesization of the research

It feels good to be pretentious.

    tl;dr

  • People feel good being knowledgeable about art and local events

  • Supporting local events is important to potential attendees

I thought hard on what made going to art shows special. The factors that go into it were endless. My partner and I used empathy mapping to really find the patterns that were prevalent. In the end, we had a few.

The most significant were:


    - Knowledge of art (context and creation)
    - Feelings of pretension, sophistication, and aesthetic fulfillment
    - Familiar mental models for the event

To answer the problems that we were facing, and to include the aspects that make art shows attractive, my partner and I designed a holistic solution to the problem, making new models for promotion, registering, attending, and reflecting.

Service-based solution

Aligning business and user goals

    tl;dr

  • Familiar structure for registering allows comfort for new events

  • Models should have elements of sustaining themselves

  • There are multiple layers to planning events of this magnitude that need to be plotted and considered

The new model we created helps bring outside potential attendees to the events by having a more robust promotion for the event, and by validating it to any potential attendee by the Eventbrite Platform as well as normal social media. This decision was based on research that younger audiences are more comfortable with a familiar model on events and promotion.





To demonstrate the use that a new service design view of the Open Studio event can offer, I chose to create a Business Model Canvas which highlights the new aspects. Here we can see the discretized system components, and the model linearly defined.



Engagement at the event

Scaffolding existing behaviors

    tl;dr

  • People take lots of pictures of art at museums and galleries

  • People like to look on those photos later for inspiration

  • An app that allows multiple ways to save art for buying or inspiration is helpful for those who need it, and unobtrusive for those who don't

When considering interaction, I looked at the model of the Cooper Hewitt Design museum and other tech-forward museums to see how interactivity is done at art exhibits. Also based on research I found that at art museums, people are likely to take photos of pieces they like, to reflect on at a later date. I designed an app that answers these habits and models; It would allow people to feel knowledgeable, to allow interaction and engagement, as well as allow for future reflection on enjoyed pieces. This app would also give artists informational data and KPI’s based on the app’s use during the spring Open Studio event. Because the behaviors around acquisition of new users for native apps is volatile, the app I designed is in a MVP state, and would be beta prototyped at the next open studio, to be further developed and designed if necessary.



Reflections

Resistance to change

    tl;dr

  • Old communities can be resistant to change, so allowing them into the process is the most important aspect

  • Supporting old faces and new connections requires many changes, but it benefits all users and stakeholders

While a new system would be highly beneficial to the HPSA and getting attendance to their events, there is a hard resistance by entrenched communities to change. My attempt was to design a way that works with the pre existing way Open Studio works, while allowing room to open up to new audiences.

At its core, the open studio is about connections. I provided a way for even more artist to attendee connections to be laid.
